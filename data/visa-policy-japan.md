# Japan Visa Policy

## Overview
Japan has visa exemption agreements with many countries. The requirements vary based on nationality and purpose of visit.

## Visa-Free Entry
Citizens of the following regions can enter Japan without a visa for tourism or business purposes:

### Short-term stays (up to 90 days)
- United States
- Canada
- United Kingdom
- Australia
- New Zealand
- Most European Union countries
- Singapore
- Hong Kong SAR
- Macau SAR

### 15-30 day stays
- Thailand (15 days)
- United Arab Emirates (30 days)
- Indonesia (15 days)
- Brunei (15 days)

## Required Documents for Entry
All visitors must present:
1. Valid passport (must be valid for duration of stay)
2. Return or onward ticket
3. Proof of sufficient funds
4. Completed arrival card (provided on flight or at airport)

## Visa Requirements for Other Nationalities
Citizens not covered by visa exemption must obtain a visa before travel:

### Tourist Visa
- Valid for single or multiple entry
- Stay duration: up to 90 days
- Required documents:
  - Passport valid for at least 6 months
  - Completed visa application form
  - Recent passport photo
  - Proof of financial means
  - Travel itinerary
  - Hotel reservations

### Business Visa
- For business meetings, conferences, negotiations
- Valid for 90 days
- Required documents:
  - All tourist visa documents, plus:
  - Letter of invitation from Japanese company
  - Certificate of company registration

## Special Entry Requirements

### Electronic Authorization (JESTA)
Starting 2025, visa-exempt visitors must obtain electronic authorization before travel:
- Apply online at least 72 hours before departure
- Valid for 2 years or passport expiry
- Fee: ¥1,000 (approximately $7 USD)
- Required information: passport details, travel information, security questions

### Working Holiday Visa
Available for citizens aged 18-30 from participating countries:
- Australia, Canada, New Zealand, UK, Ireland, France, Germany
- Allows work and travel for up to 1 year
- One-time issuance per person

## COVID-19 Related Requirements
As of 2024, COVID-19 restrictions have been lifted:
- No vaccination certificate required
- No pre-departure testing required
- No quarantine upon arrival

## Extensions and Overstays
- Extension applications must be filed at immigration office before visa expiry
- Late applications may be denied
- Overstaying penalties:
  - Fines up to ¥3,000,000
  - Detention
  - Deportation and future entry ban (1-10 years)

## Transit
Passengers transiting through Japan without leaving the airport do not need a visa if:
- Staying in the international transit area
- Transit time is less than 72 hours
- Holding confirmed onward ticket

## Important Notes
- Working on a tourist visa is strictly prohibited
- Multiple short stays to circumvent visa limits may result in entry denial
- Immigration officers have final authority on entry decisions
- Always check current requirements before travel as policies change frequently

For the most current information, visit the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan website or contact the nearest Japanese embassy or consulate.
