# Schengen Area Visa Policy

## Overview
The Schengen Area comprises 27 European countries that have abolished internal borders, allowing free movement. A single Schengen visa allows travel throughout the area.

## Schengen Member Countries
Austria, Belgium, Czech Republic, Croatia, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland

Citizens of the following countries can enter the Schengen Area without a visa for up to 90 days within any 180-day period:

## ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorization System)
Starting 2024, visa-exempt travelers must obtain ETIAS authorization:
- Apply online before travel
- Valid for 3 years or passport expiry
- Cost: €7 (approximately $8 USD)
- Processing time: Usually instant, up to 96 hours
- Multiple entries allowed
- Does not guarantee entry (border officers have final say)

## Schengen Visa Types

### Type A - Airport Transit Visa
- For passing through international zones of Schengen airports
- Does not allow entry into Schengen territory
- Required for specific nationalities

### Type C - Short-stay Visa (Tourist/Business)
Most common visa for visitors:

**Tourist Visa**
- Duration: Up to 90 days within 180-day period
- Can be single-entry, double-entry, or multiple-entry
- Required documents:
  - Valid passport (valid at least 3 months beyond stay)
  - Completed visa application form
  - Two recent passport photos
  - Travel medical insurance (minimum €30,000 coverage)
  - Proof of accommodation
  - Round-trip flight reservations
  - Proof of financial means (typically €50-100 per day)
  - Cover letter explaining purpose of visit

**Business Visa**
- Same duration as tourist visa
- Additional requirements:
  - Invitation letter from European business partner
  - Letter from employer
  - Proof of business relationship

**Visiting Family/Friends**
- Invitation letter from host
- Host's proof of residence and financial means
- Declaration of sponsorship (if host is covering expenses)

### Type D - Long-stay Visa
- For stays exceeding 90 days
- Subject to national rules of specific country
- Examples: work visa, study visa, family reunification

## Application Process
1. Determine which Schengen country to apply to:
   - Main destination (most days spent)
   - First entry point (if equal time in multiple countries)
2. Schedule appointment at embassy/consulate or visa center
3. Submit application 15 days to 6 months before travel
4. Pay visa fee (€80 for adults, €40 for children 6-12, free under 6)
5. Attend interview if required
6. Wait for processing (typically 15 days, up to 45 days in exceptional cases)

## 90/180 Day Rule
Critical rule for visa-free travelers and short-stay visa holders:
- Can stay maximum 90 days within any 180-day period
- 180-day period is a rolling window, not fixed dates
- Count backwards 180 days from any day to calculate remaining days
- Overstaying results in fines, deportation, and future entry bans

**Example:**
- Arrived: January 1
- Stayed 60 days, departed: March 1
- Can return but only for 30 more days before June 29
- After June 29, the January-March stay "drops off" and days reset

## Required Entry Documents
All visitors must show:
1. Valid passport (Schengen validity rule: valid 3 months beyond departure date, issued within last 10 years)
2. Visa or ETIAS authorization (if applicable)
3. Proof of accommodation
4. Return/onward ticket
5. Proof of sufficient funds
6. Travel insurance (for visa holders)

## Special Cases

### Digital Nomads
Some Schengen countries now offer digital nomad visas:
- Allow remote work while residing in country
- Duration: 6-12 months
- Requirements vary by country
- Examples: Portugal, Spain, Greece, Estonia

### Study Programs
Short-term study (under 90 days) may use tourist visa
Longer programs require national student visa

### Medical Treatment
Requires medical visa with:
- Letter from medical facility
- Proof of payment/insurance
- Proof of accommodation

## Common Visa Refusal Reasons
- Insufficient proof of financial means
- Questionable purpose of visit
- Inadequate travel insurance
- Passport validity issues
- Previous immigration violations
- Suspicion of intent to overstay or work illegally

## Border Crossing
- First entry must be through external Schengen border checkpoint
- Once inside, no border checks between member states
- Random checks may occur, always carry passport
- Exiting through any external border is allowed

## Working Restrictions
- Tourist and business visas do NOT permit work
- Remote work for non-EU employer is grey area (consult legal advice)
- Working holiday visas available for select countries (Australia, Canada, NZ age 18-30)

## COVID-19 Requirements
As of 2024:
- No vaccination requirements
- Individual countries may have residual health measures
- Check specific country requirements before travel

## Important Reminders
- Visa issued by one Schengen country is valid for entire area
- Border officers can deny entry even with valid visa
- Keep documentation accessible during entire stay
- Register with local authorities if required by specific country
- Overstaying has serious consequences: fines, ban (1-5 years), deportation
- Travel insurance strongly recommended even for visa-free travelers

For current information, visit official EU immigration website or consult the embassy of your main destination country.
