# Canada Visa Policy

## Overview
Canada has visa exemption agreements with many countries. Requirements vary based on nationality, purpose of visit, and whether arriving by air or land/sea.

## Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)
Citizens of visa-exempt countries arriving by air must obtain an eTA before travel. This applies to:

### eTA Required Countries (visa-exempt)
- United Kingdom
- Australia
- Japan
- South Korea
- Singapore
- Most European Union countries
- New Zealand
- Hong Kong SAR
- Israel
- Mexico
- Chile
- And many other visa-exempt countries

**Note:** U.S. citizens and travelers with a valid Canadian visa are exempt from eTA requirements.

## Visa-Free Entry
The following can enter Canada without a visa:

### No eTA or Visa Required
- United States citizens (arriving by any method)
- Valid Canadian visa holders
- Canadian permanent residents

### Land/Sea Border Entry
Citizens of visa-exempt countries arriving by land or sea do not need an eTA, but must meet all other entry requirements.

## Required Documents for Entry
All visitors must present:
1. Valid passport or travel document
2. Proof of sufficient funds for stay
3. Return or onward ticket (if applicable)
4. eTA confirmation (if arriving by air and from visa-exempt country)
5. Letter of invitation (if visiting family/friends)

## Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa)
Citizens not covered by visa exemption must obtain a visitor visa before travel:

### Tourist/Visitor Visa
- Valid for single or multiple entry
- Stay duration: up to 6 months per entry
- Required documents:
  - Passport valid for duration of stay
  - Completed application form (IMM 5257)
  - Recent passport photos (2)
  - Proof of financial support
  - Travel itinerary
  - Purpose of visit letter
  - Ties to home country (employment letter, property documents)
  - Biometrics (fingerprints and photo)

### Business Visa
- For business meetings, conferences, site visits
- Valid for up to 10 years (multiple entry)
- Required documents:
  - All tourist visa documents
  - Letter of invitation from Canadian business
  - Proof of business relationship
  - Company registration documents

### Family Visit Visa
- To visit family members in Canada
- Required documents:
  - All tourist visa documents
  - Letter of invitation from family member
  - Proof of relationship
  - Host's proof of status in Canada
  - Host's proof of financial support

## Super Visa (Parents and Grandparents)
Long-term visa for parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens or permanent residents:
- Valid for up to 10 years
- Allows stays of up to 5 years per entry
- Required documents:
  - All visitor visa requirements
  - Letter of invitation from child/grandchild
  - Proof of relationship
  - Medical insurance ($100,000 minimum coverage)
  - Child/grandchild's proof of income (meets minimum requirement)

## Transit Visa
Required for citizens of certain countries passing through Canada:
- Valid for up to 48 hours
- Required even if not leaving airport
- Free of charge
- Same documentation as visitor visa

## Processing Times
- eTA: Usually approved within minutes (up to 72 hours)
- Visitor Visa: 2-8 weeks (varies by country)
- Super Visa: 8-12 weeks
- Business Visa: 2-8 weeks

## Visa Fees
- eTA: CAD $7
- Visitor Visa: CAD $100
- Super Visa: CAD $100
- Family rate (5+ people): CAD $500
- Biometrics: CAD $85 per person

## Biometrics Requirement
Most visitor visa applicants must provide biometrics:
- Fingerprints and photograph
- Valid for 10 years
- Must be done at a Visa Application Centre (VAC)

## Extension of Stay
Visitors can apply to extend their stay in Canada:
- Must apply at least 30 days before status expires
- Fee: CAD $100
- Processing time: 4-6 months
- Maximum extension: usually 6 months

## Countries Requiring Visa
Citizens of most countries require a visa, including but not limited to:
- China
- India
- Philippines
- Nigeria
- Pakistan
- Russia
- Most African countries
- Most Asian countries
- Most Middle Eastern countries

## Special Programs
### Working Holiday Visa (IEC)
Available for youth (18-35) from participating countries for work and travel

### Study Permit
Required for academic programs longer than 6 months

## Important Notes
1. Meeting requirements does not guarantee entry
2. Border officers make final decision
3. Travelers may be refused entry even with valid visa/eTA
4. Criminal records may affect admissibility
5. Medical inadmissibility rules apply
6. Financial requirements vary by length of stay
