# Visa Requirements Reference

This document provides visa requirement information for popular travel destinations. Requirements vary by nationality - always check with the destination country's embassy or official government website for current requirements.

## Japan

### Visa-Free Entry (Tourism/Short Stay)
Citizens of the following countries can enter Japan without a visa for short stays (typically 90 days):
- Australia, New Zealand
- United States, Canada
- United Kingdom, Ireland
- Most EU countries
- Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand

### Requirements for Visa-Free Entry
- Valid passport (at least 6 months validity)
- Return or onward ticket
- Sufficient funds for stay
- No intention to work

### Tourist Visa (Required for Some Countries)
If your nationality requires a visa:
- Apply at Japanese embassy/consulate in your country
- Processing time: 5-7 business days typically
- Documents needed:
  - Completed visa application form
  - Passport photo (45mm x 45mm)
  - Valid passport
  - Flight itinerary
  - Hotel reservations
  - Proof of financial means
  - Employment letter or enrollment certificate

### Working Holiday Visa
Available for citizens aged 18-30 (or 18-25 for some countries) from:
- Australia, New Zealand, Canada
- UK, Ireland, France, Germany
- South Korea, Taiwan, Hong Kong

## Canada

### Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)
Required for visa-exempt foreign nationals flying to or transiting through Canada:
- Citizens of Australia, most EU countries, Singapore, Japan, etc.
- Apply online at official Government of Canada website
- Cost: CAD $7
- Processing: Usually within minutes (some may take days)
- Valid for 5 years or until passport expires

### Requirements for eTA
- Valid passport
- Credit or debit card for payment
- Email address

### Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa)
Required for citizens of countries not eligible for eTA:
- Apply online or at visa application center
- Processing time: Several weeks
- Documents needed:
  - Valid passport
  - Proof of financial support
  - Letter of invitation (if applicable)
  - Travel itinerary
  - Purpose of visit documentation

### Working Holiday Visa (International Experience Canada)
For young people aged 18-35 (varies by country) from eligible countries:
- Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, UK, France, Germany, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan

## United States

### Visa Waiver Program (VWP)
Citizens of 41 countries can travel without a visa for stays of 90 days or less with ESTA:
- Australia, New Zealand
- United Kingdom, Ireland
- Most EU countries
- Singapore, Japan, South Korea

### ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization)
- Apply online at https://esta.cbp.dhs.gov
- Cost: USD $21
- Processing: Usually immediate (can take up to 72 hours)
- Valid for 2 years or until passport expires
- Allows multiple entries

### B-1/B-2 Tourist Visa
Required for citizens not eligible for VWP:
- Apply at U.S. embassy/consulate
- Interview required
- Processing time: Several weeks to months
- Fee: USD $185

## Schengen Area (Europe)

### Visa-Free Entry
Citizens of many countries (including USA, Canada, Australia, Japan, Singapore) can visit without a visa for up to 90 days within any 180-day period.

### Schengen Countries Include:
Austria, Belgium, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland

### Schengen Visa (Type C)
Required for citizens not eligible for visa-free entry:
- Apply at embassy/consulate of main destination country
- Processing time: 15 days (can take up to 60 days)
- Fee: EUR 80 (adults), EUR 40 (children 6-12)
- Valid for specific duration and number of entries

## United Kingdom

### No Visa Required
Citizens of USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, and many others can visit for up to 6 months without a visa for tourism.

### Standard Visitor Visa
Required for citizens not eligible for visa-free entry:
- Apply online
- Processing time: 3 weeks typically
- Fee: GBP 100 (6 months), GBP 376 (2 years), GBP 670 (5 years), GBP 837 (10 years)

## Australia

### Electronic Travel Authority (ETA)
For short-term visits (up to 3 months) from eligible countries:
- Available for citizens of USA, Canada, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, and others
- Apply online or via app
- Cost: AUD $20
- Usually processed within 12 hours

### eVisitor (subclass 651)
Free electronic visa for European passport holders and certain other countries:
- Valid for multiple visits up to 3 months each
- Valid for 12 months from grant date

### Visitor Visa (subclass 600)
For countries not eligible for ETA or eVisitor:
- Apply online
- Processing time: Several weeks
- Fee varies by location

## General Visa Tips

1. **Check Requirements Early**: Start visa process 4-8 weeks before travel
2. **Passport Validity**: Most countries require 6 months validity beyond travel dates
3. **Multiple Entry**: Consider if you need single or multiple entry visa
4. **Transit Visas**: Some countries require visas even for airport transit
5. **Working vs. Tourist**: Never work on a tourist visa - severe penalties apply
6. **Extensions**: Research extension possibilities before arrival
7. **Overstay Penalties**: Serious consequences including future entry bans
8. **Biometric Requirements**: Many countries now require biometric data (fingerprints, photos)
9. **Travel History**: Having visas/stamps from certain countries may affect applications
10. **Insurance**: Some countries require proof of travel insurance for visa approval

## Special Visa Categories

### Working Holiday Visas
Available for young people (typically 18-30 or 18-35) from countries with bilateral agreements:
- Allows extended stays (usually 1-2 years)
- Permission to work to fund travel
- Popular programs: Australia, Canada, New Zealand, UK, Ireland, Japan

### Digital Nomad Visas
Emerging category allowing remote workers to reside long-term:
- Portugal, Estonia, Croatia, Greece, Spain
- Requirements typically include proof of remote employment and minimum income

### Student Visas
For enrolled students in educational programs:
- Requires acceptance letter from institution
- Proof of financial means
- May allow limited work hours
